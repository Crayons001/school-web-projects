Let's do trees!

Forests are vast, diverse ecosystems that cover around 31% of the Earth's land surface. They are home to countless species of plants, animals, and microorganisms, and they play a critical role in regulating the global climate by absorbing and storing carbon dioxide. Forests can be broadly classified into two types: tropical forests and temperate forests. Tropical forests are found near the equator and are characterized by high temperatures and rainfall, while temperate forests are found in areas with distinct seasons and moderate temperatures.
